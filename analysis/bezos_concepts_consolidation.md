# Bezos Letters Concept Consolidation

This is the current ruthless consolidation pass for the Bezos letters learning hub.

The original extraction produced 67 raw concepts. The hub now uses 15 parent ideas. Each parent idea keeps the merged raw concepts visible in the right-side detail pane, so the nuance is not lost.

## Merge Rules

- Merge if two concepts answer the same practical question.
- Merge if one concept is a sub-mechanism of another.
- Keep separate if the user would study it through a different deep-dive page.
- Prefer fewer front-page choices over perfect taxonomy.
- Keep business and life overlap visible instead of duplicating cards.

## Parent Ideas

### 1. Long-term ownership

Merged:
Long-term thinking, Ownership vs tenancy, Enduring franchise, Market leadership logic.

Reason:
These are all versions of the same idea: think like an owner across time and optimize for durable value rather than short-term appearance.

### 2. Day 1 anti-complacency

Merged:
Day 1 mentality, Day 2 decline, Embrace external trends, Keep a company non-typical, Differentiation is survival.

Reason:
These all warn against stasis, institutional drift, and becoming generic after success.

### 3. Customer-backed empathy

Merged:
Customer obsession as empathy, Working backwards, Be proactive before pressure, Proactive customer delight, Stable customer needs, Customer experience pillars.

Reason:
These are all ways of grounding decisions in the real person served.

### 4. High standards and craft

Merged:
High standards are teachable, High standards are domain-specific, Recognition and scope, Hidden work matters.

Reason:
All four are subparts of the 2017 high standards framework.

### 5. Decision quality and speed

Merged:
Type 1 decisions, Type 2 decisions, 70% information rule, Disagree and commit, Escalate true misalignment, One-size-fits-all decision trap.

Reason:
These all govern decision process weight, speed, reversibility, and commitment.

### 6. Judgment beyond proxies

Merged:
Resist proxies, Data plus judgment, Long-term judgment against short-term math, Heart, intuition, curiosity, taste.

Reason:
These all warn that measurable or process-based signals can become incomplete substitutes for judgment.

### 7. Invention portfolio

Merged:
Plant seeds and protect saplings, Failure is part of invention, Failure and invention are inseparable, Big winners pay for many losers, Wandering, Builder's mentality, Beginner's mind.

Reason:

### 8. Missionary value creation

### 11. Operating system of excellence

### 12. Platform and ecosystem design

### 13. Dreamy business filter

### 14. Culture as compounding infrastructure
